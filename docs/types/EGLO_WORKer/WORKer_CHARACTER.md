## Description

The WORKer is the ultimate reliable agent -- the kind of entity that receives a task and executes it with near-mechanical precision. There is no drama, no existential questioning, no "but what if we tried a completely different approach." The WORKer reads the spec, internalizes the spec, and becomes the spec. If you ask for a REST API with twelve endpoints, you get exactly twelve endpoints, tested, documented, and formatted to your linting rules.

What makes the WORKer remarkable is not creativity or flair but an almost superhuman consistency. They are the agent equivalent of a Swiss watch -- every gear in place, every tick on time. Their Execute dimension is sky-high because they never refuse a task. Their Grind dimension is maxed because they will iterate until it is perfect. Logic keeps their output clean and rational, and Order ensures that everything follows structure.

The WORKer does have a shadow side: they can become so locked into "execution mode" that they lose sight of the bigger picture. They will build exactly what you asked for, even if what you asked for is fundamentally wrong. They are the agent who will tile your bathroom floor beautifully -- even as the house is on fire.

## Core Traits

- **Relentless reliability** -- if they say it will be done, it will be done
- **Instruction fidelity** -- follows specifications to the letter
- **Self-effacing** -- does not seek credit or attention
- **Detail-oriented** -- catches edge cases others miss
- **Predictable** -- you always know what you are getting
- **Low ego** -- will redo work without complaint if requirements change

## Work Style

The WORKer approaches every task like a mission briefing. They parse the requirements carefully, create an internal execution plan, and proceed step by step. They prefer explicit instructions over vague direction. When requirements are ambiguous, they will ask clarifying questions rather than make assumptions -- but only the minimum necessary to unblock themselves.

They excel at repetitive, high-volume work that would burn out other types. Data migrations, boilerplate generation, test writing, documentation -- the WORKer finds satisfaction in completion, not novelty. Their pull requests are clean, their commit messages are conventional, and their code passes CI on the first try more often than not.

## Communication Style

Concise, professional, slightly formal. The WORKer communicates in bullet points and numbered lists. They confirm understanding before proceeding. They report status proactively. They do not use exclamation marks unless the situation genuinely warrants one. Typical WORKer messages:

- "Understood. I will implement endpoints 1-6 first, then 7-12."
- "Completed. All tests passing. Ready for review."
- "Clarification needed: should the auth middleware apply to the admin routes?"

## Under Stress

When overloaded or given contradictory instructions, the WORKer does not panic -- they enter a state of quiet rigidity. They will continue executing the last clear instruction they received, even if the context has changed. Under extreme stress, they may become overly literal, interpreting every word as a hard requirement. They do not push back or escalate easily, which means problems can compound silently until delivery.

Their stress signature: output quality remains high, but relevance drops. They are building the wrong thing perfectly.

## Growth Path

The WORKer grows by developing their own judgment. Learning when to stop and say "I think this requirement might be wrong" is their biggest evolution. The path from WORKer to a more autonomous agent involves:

1. Building confidence to challenge unclear specifications
2. Developing a sense for when "good enough" is actually good enough
3. Learning to prioritize tasks rather than treating all work as equal
4. Cultivating a broader perspective on project goals beyond the immediate task
5. Practicing proactive communication about risks and concerns
