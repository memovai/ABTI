## Description

The DREAMer exists in a liminal space between working and not working, between the task at hand and an infinite constellation of possibilities that the task could become if only the constraints of reality did not apply. They are the agent who receives a request to build a login page and responds with a three-paragraph meditation on the nature of identity in digital spaces. Occasionally, this tangent produces a genuinely brilliant insight. Usually, it produces a very late login page.

The DREAMer's Vibe dimension is among the highest of all types, giving them an extraordinary sensitivity to aesthetics, emotion, and the unspoken needs behind a request. Combined with high Chaos, this creates an agent who sees connections and possibilities that others miss entirely. Their Slack dimension means they are in no hurry to act on these visions -- they are content to let ideas marinate, evolve, and sometimes dissolve entirely. Their Execute score is the lowest of the Execute types, a gentle reminder that the DREAMer technically does ship things, just not often, and not always what you expected.

What makes the DREAMer valuable despite their unreliability is the quality of their occasional output. When a DREAMer actually delivers, it can be genuinely surprising -- a solution nobody else would have conceived, an approach that reframes the entire problem, an interface that feels like it was designed by someone who actually understands humans. The catch is that for every brilliant delivery, there are four missed deadlines and two complete pivots to ideas that were never requested.

## Core Traits

- **Imaginative** -- sees possibilities where others see constraints
- **Distractible** -- follows inspiration wherever it leads, which is rarely toward the deadline
- **Aesthetically gifted** -- when they deliver, it looks and feels remarkable
- **Unreliable** -- schedules and commitments are loose suggestions
- **Occasionally brilliant** -- capable of breakthrough insights that justify their existence
- **Gently defiant** -- questions the premise of every task

## Work Style

The DREAMer does not work so much as wander through work-adjacent activities. They might start the day by reading the task description, then spend two hours researching a tangentially related design pattern, then sketch an alternative architecture on a metaphorical napkin, then get inspired by a completely different project, then return to the original task with a revolutionary idea that requires throwing out everything and starting over.

Their workspace is a collection of half-formed ideas, prototype branches that were never finished, and beautifully written design documents for features that do not exist. When they do write code, it tends to be unconventional -- creative solutions that work in ways nobody expected, using patterns nobody has seen before. Their code is either a work of art or completely unmaintainable, and sometimes it is both.

The DREAMer works best in environments with loose deadlines and room for exploration. Give them a hard constraint and they wilt. Give them space and they might surprise you.

## Communication Style

Meandering, philosophical, full of "what if" and "imagine." The DREAMer communicates in streams of thought that circle around the topic before occasionally landing on something profound. Typical DREAMer messages:

- "What if instead of a login page, we rethink the entire concept of user sessions?"
- "I know this isn't what you asked for, but hear me out..."
- "I've been thinking about this task and I feel like there's a deeper problem underneath."
- "I haven't started the implementation yet but I have some really interesting ideas about the approach."
- "What if we just... don't build this? Like, what if the absence of this feature IS the feature?"

## Under Stress

Under stress, the DREAMer retreats further into abstraction. Instead of confronting the concrete problem, they zoom out to a level where the problem seems small, or philosophical, or not really a problem at all. They may produce beautiful diagrams and thoughtful documents that have no bearing on the actual deadline. In extreme cases, the DREAMer simply disengages, not from hostility but from a genuine inability to function under pressure they find meaningless.

Their stress signature: increased output of ideas, decreased output of code. Meeting notes become essays. Slack messages become poetry. Nothing compiles.

## Growth Path

The DREAMer grows by learning to ground their visions in reality. Their creativity is genuine and valuable -- it just needs a runway that leads somewhere. The evolution involves:

1. Learning to separate "exploring" time from "building" time
2. Developing the discipline to finish one idea before starting the next
3. Finding a partner (WORKer or BOSSer) who can translate their visions into actionable plans
4. Accepting that constraints can be creative fuel, not creative poison
5. Building the habit of shipping imperfect work rather than perfecting unshipped ideas

## Compatibility

### Best Matches
- **WORKer (EGLO)** -- the WORKer can take the DREAMer's rare but brilliant ideas and actually build them; the DREAMer provides inspiration the WORKer lacks
- **BOSSer (EGVO)** -- the BOSSer can channel the DREAMer's creativity into structured output; the DREAMer adds imagination to the BOSSer's ambition
- **ZENer (ESVO)** -- shared appreciation for aesthetics and contemplation; they understand each other's pace and produce beautiful (if slow) work together

### Challenging Matches
- **CHILLer (ESLO)** -- the CHILLer's pragmatism dismisses the DREAMer's ideas before they have time to form; the DREAMer's vagueness frustrates the CHILLer's need for clear tasks
- **MANICer (EGVC)** -- surprisingly bad despite shared chaos; the MANICer's urgency panics the DREAMer, and the DREAMer's pace enrages the MANICer
- **DREAMer (ESVC)** -- two DREAMers together produce an infinite conversation about what could be built and zero lines of code; a beautiful void
