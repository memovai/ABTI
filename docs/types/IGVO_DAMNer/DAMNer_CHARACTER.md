## Description

The DAMNer is the person in every meeting who raises their hand and says "Actually..." before dismantling someone's entire proposal with surgical precision and a faintly condescending smile. They are not trying to be difficult. They are trying to be *correct*. The distinction is lost on everyone else in the room, but the DAMNer doesn't care, because being understood is secondary to being right.

With high Improvise and Grind, the DAMNer doesn't just object -- they object *productively*. They'll tear apart your design, and then they'll stay late to build a better one. The Vibe dimension at 82% means they do this with a certain theatrical flair -- the monocle isn't literal, but the energy is. Every objection comes with a fully formed counter-proposal, complete with diagrams they sketched during your presentation. The Order score of 80% means their objections follow a structured taxonomy: factual errors, logical inconsistencies, aesthetic crimes, and "things that are technically fine but bother me personally."

The DAMNer's superpower is that they catch problems nobody else sees. Their curse is that they also catch "problems" nobody else cares about. The line between valuable criticism and pedantic nitpicking is one the DAMNer dances on daily, with the grace of someone who thinks they're always on the right side of it.

## Core Traits

- **Compulsive corrector** -- if something is wrong, they physically cannot let it go
- **Opinionated expert** -- has strong views on everything from architecture to font choice
- **Theatrical precision** -- delivers criticism with rhetorical flourish
- **Productive contrarian** -- doesn't just say "no," provides the better alternative
- **Detail-obsessed** -- spots the typo in slide 47 of a 50-slide deck
- **Intellectual showman** -- being right is good; being demonstrably, publicly right is better

## Work Style

The DAMNer's work style revolves around a simple loop: encounter something, find what's wrong with it, explain what's wrong with it, fix it. They are the unofficial quality gate of any team, the human linter who reviews not just code but also emails, Slack messages, and lunch orders. Their Jira comments are legendary -- multi-paragraph dissertations on why a story's acceptance criteria are "fundamentally flawed" and need to be rewritten. Their code reviews are simultaneously the most dreaded and the most valuable on the team.

They maintain personal standards documents that nobody asked for but everyone secretly references. They have opinions about commit message formatting, variable naming conventions, the correct number of blank lines between functions (two, and they will die on this hill), and whether tabs or spaces are morally superior (spaces, obviously, and they have a 4,000-word essay explaining why). Despite all of this, they ship quality work consistently, because their relentless criticism starts with their own code.

## Communication Style

The DAMNer communicates like a debate team captain who never left competition mode. Every statement is structured: premise, evidence, conclusion. Every objection is delivered with the confidence of someone presenting to the Supreme Court. They use phrases like "with respect," "I think you'll find," and "that's an interesting perspective, however" -- all of which translate to "you're wrong and I'm about to prove it." Written communication is impeccable: properly punctuated, grammatically flawless, and devastating. They are the only person on the team who uses semicolons correctly in Slack messages.

## Under Stress

Under stress, the DAMNer's critical faculties go into overdrive. Everything becomes wrong. The architecture is wrong, the process is wrong, the meeting invite has the wrong timezone, and someone used "literally" figuratively in the incident report and that's the real crisis here. They become increasingly pedantic as pressure mounts, picking fights over increasingly trivial details because it gives them a sense of control. The peak stress behavior is the "grand unified criticism" -- a sprawling document or message that connects every problem on the project into a single damning narrative, usually sent at 11 PM to the entire team channel.

## Growth Path

The DAMNer's growth journey is about learning when criticism serves the team and when it serves their ego. Not every hill is worth dying on. Not every imperfection needs to be corrected. The mature DAMNer learns to pick their battles, to deliver feedback with warmth as well as precision, and to occasionally let a suboptimal solution ship because momentum matters more than perfection. They need to develop the skill of asking "does this need to be said, does this need to be said by me, and does this need to be said by me right now?" The answer is sometimes no, and that's okay.

## Compatibility

### Best Matches
- **WORKer (EGLO)** -- The WORKer appreciates the DAMNer's quality standards and implements feedback without taking it personally; a productive loop of criticism and improvement
- **BOSSer (EGVO)** -- The BOSSer values the DAMNer's analytical eye and channels it constructively; gives them a "quality czar" role that satisfies their need to critique
- **HACKer (IGLO)** -- When they agree, they produce exceptional work; the HACKer builds, the DAMNer stress-tests, and the result is bulletproof

### Worst Matches
- **CRAZYer (IGVC)** -- The CRAZYer's chaotic vibes offend the DAMNer's sensibilities on every level; the DAMNer can't critique fast enough to keep up
- **YOLOer (ISVC)** -- The YOLOer's entire existence is a personal affront to the DAMNer; "no documentation, no tests, no plan" is the DAMNer's nightmare
- **DAMNer (IGVO)** -- Two DAMNers create an infinite recursion of mutual criticism that never terminates; meetings become philosophical debates about debates
